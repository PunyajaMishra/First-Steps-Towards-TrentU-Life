Step 09: Arrive to Canada

Finally, it's time to Arrive to Canada!
You have completed most of your important preparation. It is now time to finalize the packing and head for your flight. You should have booked a flight for Canada. This flight can be either direct or a connecting flight with one or more layovers. Please make sure to check the layover rules for the countries you are connecting at.

Pre Departure Check-List

A few guidelines have changed since Covid19. There are a few protocols that need to be followed. Trent University's Safe Arrival Protocol talks about the immigration protocol, trgulations and expectations for international Students after 2020. Trent's Pre Departure Check-List contains a thorough list of what to check for every week before departure. This includes documents, sim cards, covid19 tests and masks.

What to bring to residence?

If you are living in a residence, there a few thing that are very useful. Keep in mind, that these are a recommended and suggested list. If there is anything that you are unable to bring with you, no porblem, there is always a substitute or solution to it. Check out Trent's Recommended What to Bring to Residence Checklist Feel free to print it out to have it handy while completing your shopping!

Packing

Fitting your entire life in a suitcase to bring with you to a new contry is difficult and may seem like a daunting task, but it is also a chance to start fresh. Try to start packing simply for the immediate future as you will find most things in Canada. Packing your documents and essentials are important. Bear in mind that many small appliances from abroad may not work in Canada as the voltage system may be different from that of your home country.

In your Carry-On

There are some thigns that you need to keep in your carry on luggae as you will be needing these as soon as you arrive or sometime during your arrival.

  • Passport
  • Visa
  • Study Permit
  • Proof of Enrollment
  • Flight tickets
  • Canadian Currency
  • Travel International Visa Debit Card
  • Bank Balance Letter
  • Travel Insurance

Check out Step 08 for more informaiton on documents.

Items you should pack

  • Mobile with international roaming
  • Mobile Charger
  • Laptop
  • Laptop Charger
  • Converter - for voltage and plugpoint differences
  • Power Bar - recommended but not a necessity
  • Toiletries
  • Books
  • Items that remind you of home - photos/books
  • Medicines with Prescriptions if necessary
  • Light bed linens (bed sheets, pillow cases)

Seasonal Clothing

Weather can range between -40C to 40C depending on the area you are residing with. Therefore, it is important to be equipped with all types of clothes for varying seasons.

  • Spring and Summer: Light-weight clothnig is best
  • Spring and fall: Sweaters and light waterproof jackets are useful for cool and rainy days
  • Winter: Heavy Jackets, scarves, gloves, hats, waterproof boots

Packing for Winter

If you are moving from a 'not so cold' place then you will be encountering colder weather than you have experienced before. That is why it is important to pack th eright winter clothes for Canada. Again, bear in mind, winter jackets, snow boots, good thermals and gloves/hats/mufflers can be found in Canada. If you are arriving in Winter and plan to buy from Canada, it will be difficult for you to step outside without the right clothes, so please get a 'starter-kit' to continue your sopping in Canada'

  • Thermals
  • Glvoes
  • Boots
  • Jackets
  • Snow hats
  • Mufflers

Items you can buy in Canada

It is scary to move into another country all by yourself and therefore everyone wishes to get as much as they can from their home. ITs a new country and you are worried about not finding the things you need and that is completely understandable. It is recommended that you buy bulkier or heavier items once you have arrived especially if you have luggage allowance. Items that you can buy here include basic ammenities such as pillows, sheets, towels, clenaing supplies, hangers, as well as winter clothes. You can also ship a few stuff to Canada - please contact your Residence Life Coordinator if you will be living on-campus.

You can also choose to bring all these items with you instead of relying on buig once you arrive to Canada. Just make sure to follow your budget, flight luggage specifications, and your comfort as it can be a long journey.

At your Home Country's Airport

Like any other international flight, try to be on the airport at least 3 hours before your flight time. Keep your passport and all other documents in a folder and in your carry-on bag, NOT the checked-in luggage. This way it is readily available. You will need this while checking in.

At the Canadian Airport

When you arrive in Canada, you will meet a border services officer who will make sure you meet some basic requirements - they will check you official documents. Check more information on IRCC.

We got you!

Don't Stress, an adventurous but exciting journey awaits you, here in Trent University. Remeber, the University is here to help you if you have any questions. Please feel free to contact us and all the best!
