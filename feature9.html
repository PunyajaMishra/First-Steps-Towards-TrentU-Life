Step 8: Coming to Canada

Required Legal Documents

Most international students need the following documents:

  • A valid passport
  • A valid study permit
  • An entry document: either a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) or electronic Travel Authorization (eTa)

Some countries require a TRV, for a list please click here. Some countries are exempt, but you may still require an eTA if you are arriving by air, a list can be found here.

Maintaining you immigration status in Canada

You are responsible for maintaining you immigration status in Canada. A full list of conditions can be found here. Study permits must meet the following conditions:

  • be enrolled at a designated learning institution (DLI), unless exempt
  • being enrolled full-time or part-time (not recommended) during each mandatory academic term
  • make progress towards the completion of program of study
  • not taking authorized leaves longer than 150 days from study program (leave must be authorized by Trent)
  • inform IRCC each time you change post-secondary institutions
  • end your studies if no longer meet the requirements of being a student
  • leave Canada when status expires
  • other conditions noted on your study permit

If you need to extend/renew any documents, please use the guidelines below to prevent issues due to expiration or permit changes.

  • Passport: At least 6 months prior to expiration (if not more)
  • Study permit: 3-4 months prior to expiration
  • CO-OP work term permit: 3-4 months prior to expiration
  • Temporary Residence Visa (TRV): before or after the expiration date
  • Social insurance number (SIN): before the expiration date or after you extend your study permit

Health Insurance

When you study at a Ontario university, are living in the province, and are an international student you must have a primary insurance plan. The University Health Insurance Plan (UHIP) is a mandatory health plan for all international non-residents studying or working at a participating university or affiliated college in Ontario.

UHIP is eligible to:

  • International students studying in Ontario Â· international employees working in Ontario who cannot get OHIP
  • Family members (spouse and children) of international students and employees who are not eligible for OHIP
  • Canadian students and employees or other individuals who are not eligible for OHIP or are satisfying OHIP waiting period. This situation usually applies to Canadians coming from outside of Ontario or individuals having dual citizenships (e.g. US & Canada or Mexico & Canada) who are not eligible for OHIP upon returning to Ontario.
  • Short-term international visitors associated with a UHIP participating university.

For more information please see Trent University's webpage on health insurance.

Finances

Some students might have difficulty getting money to Trent from their home country because of certain regulations, Trent International can prepare a letter for your bank at home that outlines the fees that are due and how to make a payment. You can request this letter at myTrent>Academics>International>International Undergraduate Letter Request. More information can be found at Trent International's finances section.
