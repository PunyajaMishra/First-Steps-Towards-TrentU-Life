Step 05: Classes and Credits

What are Credits? What Classes Do I register for?

Now that you have activated your myTrent Portal, Trent Email address and secured a housing for yourself, its time look into the next important steps: Course Registrations. It is important to understand what your degree requirements are to be able to understand what courses you need to register for.

What are Credits? How are they different from a course?

Courses are either 0.5 credit or 1.0 credit. If a course indicates an “H” at the end of the course code, it is a half credit course (0.5). If the course indicates a “Y” at the end of the course code, it is a full credit course (1.0)

Degree Requirements

Every degree has their own " Degree Requirements" which consists of credits and courses that needs to be fulfilled in order to graduate with the said degree. A credit is a way of keeping a 'count' on these required courses for a degree and a student required a certain number of Completed Credits to graduate.

A degree requirements consists of 2 types of requirements - Program Requirements & University Requirements. It is important to keep these requirements and considerations in mind when planning your journey. All Program and University Requirements can be found on the Academic Calendar.

Planning Your Degree

Once you have an idea about all your course requirements, both program-wise and Univeristy-wise, its time to create a roadmap to graduation. So many consideration when planning your degree can be overwhelming. But not to worry! Univeristy offers many resources and services to help you throughout your Road to Success. The Planning you Degree section talks in detail about the procedure and resources to arrange your degree courses over the period of your graduation.

It is also important to recognize that not all courses are offered in every semester. Refer to the Academic Timetable to learn about what courses are offered in what semester and plan your academic expedition accordingly.

Another important service offered by the university is the Academic Advising that is dedicated to student learning, development, and success. An Academic Advisor helps you students clarify your academic goals, decide which courses to take, understand academic regulations and degree requirements, connect with the services they need and, ultimately, take charge of their own education.

Since the requirements and official methods for an international students look slightly different than that of a domestic students, the Univeristy also offers Advising for International students which focuses on support areas specific to an international student. We have the International Student Academic Advisor and International Student Immigration Advisor to guide the international students through their various questions related to academic as well as immigrations.

Course Registration

Once you have planned your degree, its time to Register for courses! Go Ahead to the Next Step (Step-06) to register for the courses.
